Interpretation
  • The Spearman ρ = +0.23 (p < 0.001) shows a statistically significant but modest positive correlation — more-vulnerable ZCTAs actually contain slightly more chargers on average.
  • This is driven by urban density confounding: California's most vulnerable populations are concentrated in dense cities (LA, SF, Fresno) that also happen to have the most chargers by absolute count.
  • The equity story lives in zero-charger ZCTAs: 50% of the least-vulnerable (Q1) ZIP areas have no public charger at all — these are car-dependent rural areas with no charging infrastructure whatsoever.
  • Only 25.7% of chargers sit inside CalEnviroScreen 4.0 DAC boundaries, despite DAC areas containing the highest-need populations by pollution and socioeconomic burden.
  • DC Fast Charger (DCFC) access is highest in the most-vulnerable quartile (56% of ZCTAs) — but these are the same dense urban ZCTAs. A per-capita analysis would likely reverse this finding.
